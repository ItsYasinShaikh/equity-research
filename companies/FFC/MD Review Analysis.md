
# CEO / MD Review Analysis

## 1. What were the biggest achievements this year?

- FFC achieved record sales revenue of **Rs 432 billion** and net profit of **Rs 73.6 billion**, with EPS increasing from **Rs 45.49 to Rs 51.69**.
- Successfully completed the first full year of the ex-FFBL integration while maintaining strong financial performance.
- Achieved high plant utilization with **112% urea** and **124% DAP** operational efficiency.
- Reduced inventory to the **lowest level in the industry** despite an oversupplied fertilizer market.
- Expanded the Sona Center retail network to strengthen farmer engagement.
- FFC was included in the **KMI-30 Index**, maintained Shariah-compliant status, and reached a record share price of approximately **Rs 591**, reflecting improved market confidence.

---

## 2. What challenges did management mention?

- Unfavourable weather conditions negatively affected agriculture.
- Weak crop yields reduced fertilizer demand.
- Farmer liquidity remained under pressure.
- The fertilizer industry experienced oversupply and elevated inventory levels.
- Higher industry-wide inventory is expected to remain a challenge into 2026.
- Gas supply arrangements for the Port Qasim plant are still in transition until new gas sources become fully available.

---

## 3. What opportunities does management see?

- New gas allocation from the Ghazij/Shawal discoveries could improve long-term production stability.
- The Pressure Enhancement Facility (PEF) is expected to support sustainable production beginning in 2026.
- Benefits from Thar Energy Limited are beginning to contribute through dividend income.
- Continued Shariah compliance may attract additional institutional and Islamic investors.
- Normalization of domestic fertilizer demand could improve sales in 2026.
- Agritech investment may strengthen long-term fertilizer supply and market stability.

---

## 4. What risks are highlighted?

- Continued high fertilizer inventories across the industry.
- Dependence on government gas allocation and infrastructure timelines.
- Agricultural demand remains sensitive to weather conditions.
- Farmer purchasing power depends on crop performance and financing availability.
- Fertilizer demand remains exposed to government agricultural policies.

---

## 5. What is management optimistic about?

- Fertilizer demand is expected to normalize.
- FFC believes it can successfully market all planned fertilizer production during 2026.
- New gas infrastructure will support future production.
- Strategic investments, particularly Thar Energy, are expected to generate increasing returns.
- The Company expects to continue creating sustainable value for shareholders while supporting national food security.

---

## 6. What facts surprised me?

- FFC achieved record profitability despite operating in an oversupplied market.
- The Company reduced inventories to the lowest level in the industry while competitors struggled with excess stock.
- Management deliberately chose **not to pass inflationary price increases to farmers**, which suggests a focus on long-term market position rather than maximizing short-term margins.
- Other income, particularly **Rs 22.4 billion in dividend income**, was a major contributor to overall performance.
- Inclusion in the KMI-30 Index and maintaining Shariah-compliant status broaden the Company's investor base.

---

# My Analyst Notes

### Does this review increase or decrease my confidence in the company?

**Confidence: Increased.**

### Why?

The CEO's Review provides evidence that FFC not only remained profitable in a difficult operating environment but also executed well operationally. The Company managed inventory better than peers, maintained exceptionally high plant utilization, expanded its retail network, and successfully integrated the ex-FFBL operations. These indicate operational strength rather than relying solely on favourable market conditions.

However, several claims require verification through the financial statements. In particular, I want to determine how much of net profit came from fertilizer operations versus investment income, whether record revenue mainly reflects the full-year consolidation of ex-FFBL or genuine underlying growth, and whether the expected benefits from gas allocation and strategic investments will translate into sustainable earnings growth.
