# FFC Dividend Analysis & Sustainability Report

## 1. Dividend History & Consistency

FFC has maintained a strong dividend-paying record, making dividends a key component of shareholder returns.

| Year | Dividend Per Share | EPS | Payout Ratio |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 2025 | Rs 37.00 | Rs 51.69 | 71.58% |
| 2024 | Rs 34.86 | Rs 45.49 | 76.63% |
| 2023 | Rs 15.49 | Rs 23.32 | 66.41% |
| 2022 | Rs 12.13 | Rs 15.76 | 76.97% |
| 2021 | Rs 14.50 | Rs 17.21 | 84.25% |

### Analyst Observation

FFC's dividend capacity has increased significantly compared with historical levels. The increase reflects a larger earnings base following the ex-FFBL integration and higher contribution from associated investments.

---

# 2. Dividend Coverage Analysis

## Earnings Coverage

Dividend Coverage Ratio:

EPS ÷ DPS

= 51.69 ÷ 37

= 1.40x

This indicates that dividends were covered by reported earnings, with approximately 28% of earnings retained within the business.

---

# 3. Cash Dividend Sustainability

Although earnings supported the dividend, cash conversion was weaker.

| Item | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Dividend Paid | Rs 70.3bn |
| Operating Cash Flow | Rs 37.0bn |
| Free Cash Flow | Rs 13.4bn |

### Analyst Observation

Operating cash flow covered approximately half of dividend payments, while free cash flow coverage was significantly lower.

The gap was mainly caused by:

- Higher inventory levels
- Increased trade receivables
- Capital expenditure requirements

However, FFC maintained liquidity through:

- Rs 181.5bn short-term investments
- Strong balance sheet position
- Diversified investment income

---

# 4. Dividend Sustainability Factors

## Positive Factors

### Market Leadership

FFC remains Pakistan's largest fertilizer producer with strong brand recognition and distribution capability.

### Diversified Income

Dividend receipts from subsidiaries and associates provide an additional earnings stream beyond fertilizer operations.

### Strong Liquidity

Large investment balances provide flexibility during temporary cash flow pressure.

---

## Risks to Future Dividends

### 1. GIDC Liability

An Rs 85bn GIDC payable remains a potential future cash obligation.

### 2. Capital Expenditure

Projects such as Gas Pressure Enhancement Facility require continued investment.

### 3. Subsidiary Dividend Dependence

A growing portion of earnings comes from investment income, making future payouts dependent on subsidiary performance.

### 4. Working Capital Normalization

Receivables and inventory levels must normalize to improve cash conversion.

---

# 5. Analyst Verdict

## Dividend Rating: Strong but Monitor

FFC remains one of the strongest dividend-paying companies on PSX due to:

- Consistent payout history
- Strong profitability
- Market leadership
- Diversified earnings base
- Large liquidity position

However, 2025 highlighted an important risk:

Reported earnings were stronger than underlying cash generation.

Future dividend sustainability depends on:

- Conversion of receivables into cash
- Successful completion of CAPEX projects
- Continued subsidiary dividend flows

## Investment Conclusion

FFC remains suitable as a core dividend holding for long-term investors. The company’s financial strength supports continued payouts, but investors should monitor cash flow recovery rather than relying only on reported earnings.
