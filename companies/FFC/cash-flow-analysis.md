# Cash Flow Analysis

## Company
**Fauji Fertilizer Company Limited (FFC)**

**Financial Year:** 2025

---

---

# Operating Cash Flow

## Key Findings

- Operating cash flow declined from **Rs 106.4 billion** to **Rs 37.0 billion**.
- Operating cash flow represented approximately **50%** of reported net profit.
- Working capital consumed significant cash during the year.

### Major Working Capital Drivers

- Increase in trade receivables.
- Higher inventory levels, particularly DAP.
- Changes in other working capital balances.

### Analyst View

The decline in operating cash flow appears to be driven primarily by working capital requirements rather than a collapse in profitability. Future periods should be monitored to determine whether these working capital pressures reverse as inventories normalize and receivables are collected.

---

# Investing Cash Flow

## Key Findings

- Capital expenditure increased substantially to approximately **Rs 23.6 billion**.
- Major investments focused on production infrastructure, including the Pressure Enhancement Facility (PEF).
- Additional investment was made in Agritech Limited.
- Dividend income from subsidiaries and associates helped offset investment-related cash outflows.

### Analyst View

Investment activity indicates management is allocating capital toward expanding production capacity and strengthening long-term competitiveness rather than maintaining existing assets alone.

---

# Financing Cash Flow

## Key Findings

- New long-term borrowings of approximately **Rs 30 billion** were raised.
- Long-term debt repayments continued during the year.
- Record cash dividends of approximately **Rs 70.3 billion** were distributed to shareholders.

### Analyst View

FFC continued rewarding shareholders while financing long-term strategic investments. The balance between dividend distributions, internal cash generation, and external financing should be monitored over future reporting periods.

> **Note:** FFC presents an internal free cash flow measure using a different methodology. The calculation above follows the conventional analyst definition: Operating Cash Flow minus Capital Expenditure.

---

# Earnings Quality

## Does cash flow support reported earnings?

Reported net profit reached **Rs 73.6 billion**, while operating cash flow totaled **Rs 37.0 billion**.

This gap appears to be largely explained by significant working capital movements, particularly higher receivables and inventory.

### Analyst View

Earnings quality appears weaker than in the previous year because accounting profits were not fully converted into operating cash flow. At this stage, there is insufficient evidence to conclude that this deterioration is structural. Future reporting periods should confirm whether cash conversion improves as working capital normalizes.

---

# Strengths

- Positive operating cash flow.
- Positive free cash flow.
- Continued investment in productive assets.
- Strong dividend income from subsidiaries.
- Ability to finance strategic expansion.

---

# Areas for Monitoring

- Cash conversion from earnings.
- Collection of trade receivables.
- Inventory normalization.
- Sustainability of subsidiary dividend income.
- Balance between dividend payments and internally generated cash.

---

# Investment Conclusion

The Cash Flow Statement presents a more balanced picture than the Income Statement alone.

While FFC reported record profitability, operating cash generation declined significantly due to working capital pressures. Nevertheless, the Company remained cash-generative, produced positive free cash flow, continued investing in long-term productive assets, and maintained substantial shareholder distributions.

The key investment question is whether the weaker cash conversion observed in 2025 represents a temporary consequence of inventory and receivable movements or the beginning of a longer-term trend. This should be closely monitored in future annual reports.

Overall, the cash flow statement moderately supports the investment case but highlights the importance of improving cash conversion to sustain long-term dividend growth and financial flexibility.
