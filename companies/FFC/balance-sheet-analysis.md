# Financial Position Assessment

## 1. Liquidity

### Strengths

- FFC maintains a strong liquidity position despite lower cash balances.
- Short-term investments of approximately **Rs 181.5 billion** provide substantial financial flexibility.
- Positive working capital indicates sufficient capacity to meet short-term obligations.

### Analyst View

Liquidity does not appear to be a concern. The decline in cash balances reflects capital allocation decisions rather than financial stress.

---

## 2. Debt Position

### Observations

- Short-term borrowings decreased by more than 40%.
- Long-term borrowings increased significantly to finance strategic projects.
- Interest coverage remains strong, suggesting debt servicing capacity is comfortable.

### Analyst View

Debt has shifted toward long-term financing, which better aligns with long-term capital investments. Current leverage appears manageable, but future returns on these investments should be monitored.

---

## 3. Asset Allocation

Management continues investing in long-term productive assets.

Key areas include:

- Pressure Enhancement Facility (PEF)
- Production infrastructure
- Strategic investment in Agritech Limited
- Existing investments in subsidiaries and associates

### Analyst View

Capital allocation appears focused on supporting future production capacity and strengthening FFC's long-term competitive position.

---

## 4. Capital Structure

FFC finances a significant portion of its assets through operating liabilities, particularly trade and other payables, while maintaining a moderate level of financial debt relative to earnings and cash generation.

### Analyst View

The Company's capital structure appears balanced. Strong profitability and cash generation provide confidence that debt obligations remain manageable.

---

## 5. Areas for Monitoring

### Trade Receivables

Trade receivables increased significantly during the year.

Possible reasons include:

- Higher credit sales
- Timing differences in collections
- Full-year consolidation of ex-FFBL
- Changes in dealer financing

Further analysis of the receivables notes is required before determining whether this represents increased credit risk.

---

### Inventory

Inventory increased materially during 2025.

Management attributes this primarily to weak DAP demand.

Future analysis should assess:

- Inventory turnover
- Potential pricing pressure
- Margin impact if excess inventory persists

---

### Long-term Borrowings

Long-term borrowings increased to support capital expenditure.

The key question is whether these investments generate higher future production, profitability, and shareholder returns.

---

# Overall Assessment

## Strengths

- Strong liquidity position
- Significant strategic investments
- Reduced short-term debt
- Healthy equity base
- Continued investment in productive assets

## Points Requiring Monitoring

- Higher trade receivables
- Increased inventory
- Rising long-term borrowings
- Execution of ongoing capital projects

---

# Investment Conclusion

FFC's balance sheet remains financially strong despite changes in its asset mix.

The Company deployed capital toward long-term production assets while reducing short-term borrowings and maintaining substantial liquid investments. Although long-term debt increased to finance strategic projects, leverage remains manageable given FFC's profitability, interest coverage, and strong credit profile.

The primary areas requiring continued monitoring are the sharp increase in trade receivables, higher inventory levels, and the successful execution of major capital projects. At present, these developments appear linked to expansion initiatives and prevailing market conditions rather than signs of financial distress.

Overall, the balance sheet supports management's strategy of sustaining production, maintaining dividend capacity, and investing for long-term growth.
